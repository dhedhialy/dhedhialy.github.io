MIT Critical Data

Hospital-Invariant ICU Representation Learning

IEEE CIBCB 2026 Submission  ·  March 2026

An end-to-end ICU mortality prediction pipeline built on MIMIC-IV, processing high-dimensional tabular features from clinical time-series and laboratory data. The pipeline spans data extraction, feature engineering, model evaluation, interpretability, and unsupervised patient phenotyping.

Results: Evaluated Logistic Regression, Random Forest, XGBoost, and LightGBM under class imbalance — achieving AUROC > 0.84 and AUPRC ≈ 0.28. Applied GroupKFold cross-validation for patient-level separation. Optimized thresholds for high-recall performance (≈ 0.85) via PR curve analysis. MiniBatchKMeans clustering with cluster-specific SHAP analysis to uncover heterogeneous mortality drivers. UMAP for low-dimensional validation of latent patient structure.

BBB-Penetrant Peptide Design for EGFRvIII Targeting

Original molecule designed by Aly Dhedhi  ·  November 2025 – April 2026

A cloud-native in silico pipeline for designing blood-brain barrier (BBB) penetrant peptides targeting EGFRvIII in glioblastoma. Four Angiopep-2/GE11 fusion constructs were designed with structural variations — cyclization, terminal capping, and retro-enantio — to probe stability and binding geometry. Structures were generated via ESMFold, docked with PatchDock, and scored using a custom empirical interface scoring function.

Lead candidate (P3 — Cyclized): ≈ 281 hydrophobic contacts and 33 salt bridges at the receptor binding interface. Highest BBB penetration likelihood across all four constructs based on physicochemical profiling (charge, MW, GRAVY).

The Citizens Foundation (TCF)

Fundraising & Public Advocacy

The Citizens Foundation (TCF) is one of Pakistan's largest non-profit organizations, founded in 1995 with a mission to provide quality education to children from the most underserved communities across the country. Operating over 1,700 schools and reaching more than 300,000 students, TCF addresses the systemic barriers — poverty, gender inequality, and geographic isolation — that keep millions of children out of classrooms. Each school is built in low-income settlements and staffed by trained, locally recruited female teachers, creating a model that is both scalable and community-rooted.
